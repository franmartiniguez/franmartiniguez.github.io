Cameraman

In order to choose the edges for the first picture of the cameraman, I just tried to get as many of his body and his camera as possible, as those are the main edges, then I tried to add some of the background, but to varying degrees of success. When I convolved with the Gaussian filter, it made it nicer in terms of getting rid of some of the background noise, but it also made it harder in terms of getting the best edges. I settled on a tradeoff where you can still clearly see his body, but there are some edges were there is still signifcant noise. Also notice that there is no difference between doing the Gaussian and finite difference operators as one or two convolutions.

Part 2

Sharpening

Here I implemented an unsharp mask filter. Basically what it does is blur the image to find the low frequency parts of an image and then subtracts those from the image. From this, we obtain the high frequencies of the image which we can add back on to the image to make it sharper. As you can see varying the amount of sharpness makes somewhat of a change, but not a very pronounced one, at least not when you don't have a lot of original amount of high frequencies to work with like in the blurred cat example.
